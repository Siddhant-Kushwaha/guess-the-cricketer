How to play Guess the Cricketer

Guess the Cricketer is a daily cricket guessing game — think of it as a cricket wordle for the sport we actually argue about. Every day there is one mystery cricketer to identify, and you get eight guesses to name them. Type any player from the pool and the game grades seven attributes with coloured tiles.

  • Green (exact) — that attribute is a perfect match.
  • Yellow (close) — you are warm: the country is in the same region, the role is related (an all-rounder is close to a batter or bowler), the bowling is the same family (pace vs spin), or a number is within three.
  • Grey (wrong) — no match on that attribute.
  • ▲ / ▼ arrows — on debut year and shirt number, they tell you whether the answer is higher or lower than your guess.

The seven feedback columns are country, role, batting hand, bowling style, debut year, IPL team and shirt number. Cross-reference the clues from each guess to narrow the field — a green country plus a yellow role plus a "debut ▼" tells you a lot.

IPL mode vs Legend mode

IPL mode is the easy front door: the answer is always a cricketer who has played in the Indian Premier League, so every name is instantly recognisable. Legend mode opens the full cross-era pool — Bradman, Sobers, Kapil Dev and Wasim Akram sit alongside today's stars — for players who want a proper challenge. There is also an unlimited practice mode that serves a fresh random cricketer whenever you want and never touches your daily streak.

Share and streaks

When you finish, tap share to post a spoiler-free emoji grid — just the tile colours, the puzzle number and your streak — straight to WhatsApp or any app. Your streak, win rate and archive are saved on your device, so come back tomorrow for a new cricketer and keep the run alive.
